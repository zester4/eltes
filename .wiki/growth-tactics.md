# Growth Tactics

*Proven acquisition channels, referral mechanics, community-led growth, and launch playbooks. What works in 2025-2026 for B2B and B2C.*

---

## Channel Selection Framework

Not all channels work for all businesses. Pick 1-2 channels and go deep before adding more. Spreading across 5 channels at 20% effort each is how you fail at all of them.

**How to pick your channel:**
1. Where does your ICP already spend time? (Fish where the fish are)
2. What do you have unfair advantage in? (Network, content, distribution)
3. What's your CAC tolerance vs. LTV? (Some channels cost more but convert better)
4. How fast do you need results? (Paid = fast, SEO = slow, community = medium)

**Channel speed vs. scale:**
| Channel | Time to first result | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| Paid social | Days | High (budget-limited) |
| Cold outbound | Week | Medium (effort-limited) |
| Content/SEO | 3-6 months | Very high |
| Community | 3-6 months | Medium-high |
| Referral/viral | Variable | Very high |
| Partnerships | 1-3 months | High |
| Product Hunt | One day | Medium (one-time spike) |

## Referral Programs That Actually Work

**The mechanics of a working referral program:**

1. **Both-sided reward** — giver and receiver both get something (Dropbox: storage for both; Uber: credit for both)
2. **Friction-free sharing** — pre-written message + unique link, one click to share
3. **Reward matches the product** — give more of your product, not generic gift cards
4. **Trigger at the right moment** — after first success, not on signup; after a win, not day 1
5. **Visible progress** — show users how close they are to the reward

**When to NOT do referral:** If your product isn't yet "worth sharing" — a poor product + referral program just accelerates negative word of mouth.

**Referral conversion rate benchmarks:**
- Under 1% share rate: program has a friction problem or wrong trigger moment
- 1-5% share rate: normal for B2B
- 5%+ share rate: product is genuinely loved AND referral is well-designed

## Community-Led Growth

Community works for products where people get more value from connection than from the software itself. SaaS, creator tools, professional development, niche B2B.

**The 5 stages of community building:**

1. **Seed (0-50 members):** Handpick founding members. Personally invite people who are already talking about the problem. No automation — pure high-touch.
2. **Build (50-200):** Create rituals. Weekly event, recurring format, regular "why we're here" post. Create the minimum structure.
3. **Sustain (200-1000):** Identify 10-15 "super members" who participate most actively. Give them recognition, early access, or a role. They do more than you realize.
4. **Scale (1000+):** Sub-communities by role, interest, or geography. A 10,000 person community with no structure is dead.
5. **Monetize:** Members pay for deeper access, premium events, certification, or job placement. Never paywall the core community.

**Community platform selection:**
- Discord: Best for developer, gaming, creator communities. High engagement, lower signal-to-noise
- Slack: Best for professional B2B communities. Lower engagement ceiling but more serious members
- Circle/Mighty Networks: Best for paid communities, courses + community combos
- Reddit: Never try to "own" a subreddit — contribute to existing ones instead

## Product Hunt Launch Playbook

**The only metric that matters:** #1 product of the day. Anything less is noise.

**Pre-launch (3 weeks before):**
- Choose launch day: Tuesday or Wednesday (Monday is slow, Thursday/Friday fade fast)
- Find a hunter with 1000+ followers who hunts your category — DM them personally with a specific, non-generic ask
- Create the assets: 1 hero image (the scroll-stopper), 3-5 gallery images, 60-second demo video, tagline under 60 chars
- Set up a "coming soon" page on PH; start collecting followers weeks before
- Engage in PH comments on other products so you have history

**Launch day (12:01am PST):**
- Post at exactly 12:01am PST — the daily ranking resets
- Email your entire list at 8am PST US time with a direct link and one-tap "upvote" CTA (never say "upvote" — say "check it out" or "support us")
- Post in relevant Slack groups and Discord servers at 9am PST
- Twitter/LinkedIn posts at 9am and 3pm PST
- Respond to every comment within 30 minutes all day — comment velocity is a ranking signal
- Have team members share in their personal networks staggered through the day

**Maker comment:** Write a personal, story-driven maker comment that explains why you built this. This is your one-time chance to be human in front of 50,000 people.

## LinkedIn Organic Growth Playbook (for founders/executives)

**Phase 1 — Consistency (month 1-2):**
- Post 4x/week minimum; reply to every comment within 2 hours
- Comment on 10 other posts per day (genuine, specific, adds value)
- Follow and engage with 20 people in your target audience per day

**Phase 2 — Voice (month 2-4):**
- Find the 3 topics you have strongest opinions on
- Write one "hot take" post per week — your real opinion, clearly stated
- Start sharing results and metrics (even uncomfortable ones)

**Phase 3 — Amplification (month 4+):**
- Repurpose your top 5 posts from phase 2 in different formats (carousel, video, text post)
- Collaborate with others in your space — tag them, do joint posts, interview them
- Use polls to generate engagement and signal from your audience

**Growth benchmarks:**
- Month 1-3: 0-500 followers (building foundation)
- Month 3-6: 500-2000 (gaining momentum)
- Month 6-12: 2000-10,000 (if consistent and growing)

## Growth Hacking Patterns That Work

**The Lindy effect for growth:** Tactics that have worked for 10+ years still work — just at different scale/cost. Don't overcomplicate it.

**Acquisition patterns with highest ROI:**
1. **The "Powered by" footer:** Viral loop at zero cost. Works if the product produces output others see. (Typeform, Hotjar, Canva all built on this)
2. **Reverse trial:** Give full product for 14 days, not a limited free tier. Much higher conversion than freemium.
3. **Win competitor's unhappy customers:** Search G2/Trustpilot for 2-3 star reviews of competitors. These people are in market and frustrated. Reach out personally.
4. **Co-marketing with adjacent products:** Find products with same ICP but different functionality. Joint webinar → shared lead list. Email swap → each recommends the other.
5. **Guest post on high-authority sites:** One article on HubSpot blog or TechCrunch drives more qualified traffic than 50 posts on your own site.
6. **Build a tool that solves a specific problem for free:** A free tool generates backlinks, email signups, and brand awareness at scale. (CoSchedule headline analyzer, HubSpot's website grader)

## Metrics That Matter by Stage

| Stage | North Star Metric | Leading Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-PMF | Weekly active users returning | Retention, NPS |
| Early growth | MoM revenue growth | CAC, payback period |
| Scale | Net revenue retention | Expansion MRR, churn |
| Mature | Market share | LTV/CAC ratio |

**Vanity metrics to ignore at early stage:** Total signups (without activation), page views, social followers, app downloads without DAU data.
