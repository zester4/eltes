# Content Creation

*How to create content that grows audiences, drives engagement, and converts. Platform-specific formats, virality mechanics, content strategy, and repurposing.*

---

## What Actually Goes Viral

Virality is not random. Content spreads when it triggers one or more of these:

| Trigger | Mechanism | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Identity** | "This is me" — people share to express who they are | "If you do X, you're a real Y" |
| **Useful** | Saves time, solves a problem, teaches something | Step-by-step guides, tools, templates |
| **Outrage** | Moral indignation or injustice | "This is wrong and here's why" |
| **Awe** | Something extraordinary or beautiful | Stats that reframe your worldview |
| **Curiosity gap** | Needs to be resolved | "The reason [unexpected thing] is happening" |
| **Insider knowledge** | Makes sharer feel smart for knowing | "[Industry secret] most people don't know" |
| **Counterintuitive** | Contradicts common belief | "Stop doing [conventional advice]" |
| **FOMO** | Fear of missing out | "Everyone I know is doing X" |

**Rule:** A piece of content should trigger at least 2 of these to have real viral potential.

## Platform-Specific Formats

### LinkedIn

**What works:**
- **Personal narrative posts** — "I [did something] and learned [counterintuitive thing]"
- **Listicles with specifics** — "7 things I wish I knew before [X]. Number 4 changed everything."
- **Contrarian takes** — Disagree with conventional wisdom in your industry
- **Behind the scenes** — Real numbers, real failures, real process
- **Carousel documents** — 8-12 slides, lead slide is the hook, last slide is CTA

**Format rules:**
- Line 1 = hook (see copywriting wiki)
- Line 2 = blank line (forces "see more" click)
- Short paragraphs — 1-3 lines maximum
- End with a question to drive comments
- Post at 7-9am or 5-7pm Tuesday-Thursday for best reach

**Avoid:** company updates, press release tone, "Excited to announce", stock photos

### Twitter / X

**What works:**
- **Thread openers:** "X things I learned from [credible source/experience]:" — drives thread reads
- **Hot takes:** Short, provocative, quotable — one sentence that makes people stop
- **Useful threads:** Step-by-step how-to, resource lists, explainers with examples
- **Reply game:** Reply to large accounts with genuinely valuable additions — earns follows from their audience

**Format rules:**
- Tweets under 240 characters perform better than max-length
- Images increase reach; screenshots of interesting data especially
- Threads should have a payoff — deliver on the promise of the hook
- "Tweet and forget" rhythm: post 3-5x/day, don't obsess over individual performance

### Instagram / Reels

**What works:**
- **Hook in first frame and first spoken word** — both simultaneously
- **Value-first format:** Give the tip immediately, don't tease it
- **Trending audio + original content** — relevance signal to algorithm
- **Text overlay on key points** — most watch without sound
- **Pattern interrupt in first 0.5s:** Jump cut, unexpected visual, text that confuses before it resolves

**Carousel format (feed):**
- Slide 1: Hook — bold claim or intriguing question
- Slides 2-8: One point per slide, dense value
- Last slide: CTA + save prompt

### TikTok

**Format rules:**
- Open mid-sentence or mid-action — no intro, no "hey guys"
- Sound is primary — audio hook as important as visual
- 7-21 seconds performs best for reach; 60s+ for deeper niche
- Comment reply videos dramatically boost reach
- Native text overlays and effects > imported polished content

**Content types that work:**
- "POV:" format — places viewer in a scenario
- "Things [X type of person] does" — identity triggers
- Unfiltered opinions about industry topics
- Day-in-the-life with honest commentary
- "Replying to @user" — answers to questions from comments

## Content Pillars System

Don't post randomly. Every account needs 3-5 content pillars that everything maps to.

**How to define pillars:**
1. What does your brand stand for? (core belief)
2. What does your audience need most? (utility)
3. What's your unfair knowledge advantage? (authority)
4. What makes you relatable? (personality)
5. What are you selling? (conversion)

Ratio: 70% value/entertainment, 20% proof/authority, 10% direct offer

## The Content Calendar (Minimal Viable Version)

Weekly rhythm that works:
- **Monday:** Educational (teach one thing clearly)
- **Wednesday:** Story (narrative with a lesson)
- **Friday:** Proof (result, case study, testimonial)
- **Daily:** Engagement (reply, comment, reshare others' content with your take)

Quarterly: One major "tent pole" piece — a deep guide, research report, or large resource — that earns links, shares, and email signups.

## Repurposing Methodology

One core idea → many pieces of content. Never create once.

**Repurposing tree:**
```
Long-form article / research report
├── LinkedIn carousel (key stats + insight)
├── Twitter thread (one insight per tweet)
├── Instagram carousel (most visual points)
├── Short-form video (hook + 60-second summary)
├── Email newsletter (full version + personal take)
└── Podcast/audio talking points
```

**The atomic content unit:** Break everything into the smallest useful piece. A case study becomes: headline stat (Twitter), full narrative (LinkedIn), visual before/after (Instagram), quote pull (multiple platforms).

## Writing for Engagement

**Opening hooks by platform:**

LinkedIn: Personal, specific, stakes-raising
> "I turned down a $2M contract last year. Here's why it was the right call."

Twitter: Provocative or useful
> "Everyone talks about work-life balance. Nobody talks about the cost of achieving it."

Email subject line: Curiosity or personal
> "Quick question" / "You're probably doing this wrong" / "Re: [their industry]"

**The comment trigger:** End every piece of content with a question that's:
- Easy to answer (yes/no or short answer)
- Related to their experience, not your product
- Genuinely interesting to hear the answer to

## SEO-Driven Content Creation

**Keyword intent matching:**
- Informational intent → educational content, guides
- Commercial intent → comparisons, reviews, "[X] vs [Y]"
- Transactional intent → product pages, landing pages, demos
- Navigational intent → brand terms, never try to "steal" these

**Winning structure for SEO content:**
1. Answer the query immediately in paragraph 1 (featured snippet targeting)
2. Go deeper with context in the body
3. Use H2/H3 headings that match long-tail variations of the keyword
4. Include FAQ section at the bottom targeting "people also ask" queries

**AI-first SEO (2025 onwards):**
AI overviews cite sources that: have clear authorship, use structured data, answer questions directly, have high domain authority in the topic area. Write for LLMs that will cite you, not just humans who will click. This means: clear topic sentences, direct answers before elaboration, factual claims with citations.

## Content Quality Bar

Before publishing, every piece passes this:
- [ ] Does it deliver on the headline's promise?
- [ ] Is the first line a hook that earns the read?
- [ ] Would I read this if someone else posted it?
- [ ] Is every paragraph pulling its weight or can it be cut?
- [ ] Is there a single clear action or takeaway?
- [ ] Have I removed every piece of fluffy language?
- [ ] Does the format match the platform?
